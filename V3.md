## 2. Product Vision (Owner's Words — Structured)

> A bookkeeping software specifically for bookkeeping.

The owner needs to record **every day's money movement** clearly:

1. **Cash income** and **cash expense**
2. **Bank income** and **bank expense**
3. **Daily sales bills** (money owed by customers / received from customers)
4. **Daily purchase bills** (money owed to suppliers / paid to suppliers)
5. **General receipts & payments** (not always tied to a sales/purchase bill)

Each transaction must have **clear details** and land in the **correct ledger** automatically after save.

The interface must be **simple but effective** so daily ledgers are easy to track.

## 3. Core Entry Types

### 3.1 Sales Bill (Customer / Sales Party)

**Flow — fields in this order:**

| Step | Field | Required | Notes |
|------|-------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Invoice number | Yes | Fixed prefix **`ISHABA`** + owner types the rest manually (e.g. `ISHABA-1042`) |
| 2 | Date | Yes | Bill date |
| 3 | Party name | Yes | Type-ahead dropdown of existing customers; create new party on save if not found |
| 4 | Party GST number | No | Optional; auto-fills when party is selected if already saved |
| 5 | Amount | Yes | Bill total |
| — | Save | — | Entry posts to **customer ledger** automatically |

**Party memory:** When a party already exists, selecting them auto-fills GST number and saved details.

**Ledger effect:** Sales bill increases what the customer owes (debit to customer) unless payment is collected in the same step (future: partial payment on bill — see §3.4).

---

### 3.2 Purchase Bill (Supplier / Purchase Party)

**Flow — same pattern as sales:**

| Step | Field | Required | Notes |
|------|-------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Invoice number | Yes | Supplier's bill / our reference number |
| 2 | Date | Yes | |
| 3 | Party name | Yes | Supplier autocomplete |
| 4 | Party GST number | No | Optional; auto-fill from saved supplier |
| 5 | Amount | Yes | |
| — | Save | — | Entry posts to **supplier ledger** automatically |

---

### 3.3 General — Receipt or Payment

A separate section called **"General"** for money that may or may not link to a party.

#### Step 1: Receipt or Payment?

- **Receipt** = money in  
- **Payment** = money out  

#### Step 2: Cash or Bank?

- **Cash** → record in cashbook  
- **Bank** → ask sub-mode:
  - **UPI**
  - **Net banking**

#### Step 3: Party or Indirect?

| Option | Behaviour |
|--------|-----------|
| **Party linked** | Type party name → autocomplete → amount updates **party ledger** |
| **Indirect Income** (receipt only) | No party — general income line in cashbook |
| **Indirect Expense** (payment only) | No party — general expense line in cashbook |

#### Step 4: Remark (always available)

Free-text remark shown **next to the ledger entry**.

Examples:
- Receipt + Indirect Income + remark *"Sold used oil ₹1,000"* → ledger shows **Indirect Income** with that remark
- Payment + Indirect Expense + remark *"Electrical work in workshop ₹1,000"* → ledger shows **Indirect Expense** with that remark

---

### 3.4 Outstanding / Partial Settlement Logic

When a **receipt** or **payment** is linked to a party:

> If a party owed ₹1,000 and they paid ₹500, the ledger must show ₹500 received against ₹1,000 — **₹500 still outstanding**.

Same logic for:
- Customer receipts (against sales bills / running due)
- Supplier payments (against purchase bills / running payable)
- Cash or bank — only the **mode** changes, ledger math stays the same

**Display rules:**
- Running balance per party (Dr/Cr or plain "Due" / "Advance" — keep Shahin-style readable labels)
- Each receipt/payment line shows amount, mode (cash / UPI / net banking), and remaining outstanding

---

### 3.5 Journal — Discount, Credit Note, Debit Note

A separate **Journal** action on Home for adjustments that are not plain receipts/payments.

#### Discount settlement (payment + discount = full bill clearance)

| Type | Party | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Discount given** | Customer | Customer pays less than bill due; discount + payment clears the bill |
| **Discount received** | Supplier | We pay less than bill due; discount + payment clears the bill |

**Rule:** `Payment + Discount = Bill due` (discount must be less than due — use a credit note for a full waiver).

- Payment portion → cash/bank book + party ledger
- Discount portion → party ledger only (no cash movement)

#### Credit / Debit notes (customer or supplier)

| Note | Party | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Credit note** | Customer or supplier | Reduces outstanding on a linked bill (no payment) |
| **Debit note** | Customer or supplier | Increases outstanding on a linked bill |

- Customer notes: `CN-0001`, `DN-0001`
- Supplier notes: `SCN-0001`, `SDN-0001`

**Corrections:** Journal entries are editable from the party statement (discount settlements update payment + discount together; standalone discount-linked payments cannot be edited separately).

## 4. Daily Books Structure

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│  HOME / TODAY'S BOOK                                    │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  [ + Sales Bill ]  [ + Purchase Bill ]  [ + General ]   │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Cash book (today)     │  Bank book (today)             │
│  ─ receipts            │  ─ receipts (UPI / NB)         │
│  ─ payments            │  ─ payments (UPI / NB)         │
│  ─ running balance     │  ─ running balance             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│  Party ledgers (link from Customers / Suppliers)        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Cash book tracks

- Cash receipts (party or indirect income)
- Cash payments (party or indirect expense)
- Running cash balance for the day / period

### Bank book tracks

- Bank receipts — UPI or net banking
- Bank payments — UPI or net banking
- Running bank balance

### Party ledgers track

- Opening balance (B/F)
- Sales bills / purchase bills
- Receipts & payments against that party
- Closing outstanding

## 5. Invoice Number Rule

- **Sales bills** always use prefix **`ISHABA`**
- UI shows `ISHABA` as fixed prefix; user types only the suffix (e.g. `1042` → stored as `ISHABA-1042` or `ISHABA1042` — decide one format in implementation)
- Purchase bills use supplier's invoice number as entered (no forced prefix)

## 6. Party Autocomplete (Shared Pattern)

Used in: Sales bill, Purchase bill, General receipt/payment (when party linked).

**Behaviour:**
1. User types in party name field
2. Dropdown shows matching existing parties (customers or suppliers depending on context)
3. Select → auto-fill GST + saved details
4. No match → on save, create new party master record
5. Next time that name appears in dropdown

**Technical note:** Reuse existing `entity-picker` utilities (`filterEntitiesByQuery`, `findEntityByQuery`, `isNewEntityName`) from quick-bill modal.

## 10. UI Principles

1. **One action per modal** — no "quick bill vs GST invoice" fork
2. **Field order matches how the owner thinks** — invoice # → date → party → GST (optional) → amount → save
3. **Autocomplete everywhere** for party names
4. **Remarks visible in ledger** — never hidden in a detail drawer only
5. **Outstanding always visible** after partial receipt/payment
6. **Mobile-friendly** web (owner may use phone) but desktop-first data entry
7. **CSS variable colors only** — see `docs/11_THEMING_AND_COLORS.md`
